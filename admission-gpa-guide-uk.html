UK University Admission: GPA Requirements and Grade Conversion

What GPA UK universities look for, how they evaluate international grades from the US, India, Pakistan and Australia, and how to present your converted grades in a UCAS application.

UK entry requirements by programme type

UK universities specify entry requirements in degree-classification terms (First, 2:1, 2:2). The table below shows how international GPAs typically map to those requirements.

How UK universities evaluate international grades

Most UK universities maintain their own country-specific equivalency tables, often based on guidance from UK ENIC (the national agency for international qualifications). The process for each major sending country is:

  • USA: A 4.0 GPA is read directly. 3.7+ = First, 3.0–3.7 = 2:1, 2.0–3.0 = 2:2.
  • India: A CGPA of 7.5+/10 or 70%+ percentage is typically a First equivalent. 6.0–7.49/10 = 2:1.
  • Pakistan: Similar to India — 3.5+/4.0 CGPA = First equivalent at most UK universities.
  • Australia: HD average = First; strong D average = 2:1; C average = 2:2.

Applying through UCAS

UCAS is the centralised application system for UK undergraduate admissions. International applicants submit through UCAS and include transcripts and predicted grades. GPA is not entered into UCAS directly — the form captures qualifications and grades from your home system, and the university admissions team makes the equivalency judgment.

Frequently asked questions

Do UK universities look at GPA for international applicants?

UK universities understand that many international students will not have a UK-style degree classification. They assess international qualifications through their own equivalency tables or through services like UK ENIC (formerly NARIC). Presenting a converted GPA alongside your original percentage or grade is helpful but should be accompanied by an official transcript.

What do UK universities consider a 'good' international GPA?

For a standard Bachelor's programme, the equivalent of a UK 2:1 (approximately 3.0–3.7 US GPA or 60–69% in UK terms) is the benchmark for most taught programmes. For competitive Master's programmes at Russell Group universities, a First-class equivalent (3.7+ GPA / 70%+ UK equivalent) is typically expected.

How does UCAS points relate to GPA?

UCAS (the UK's university application system) uses a points tariff based on A-Level and equivalent qualifications — not GPA. A-Level grades are the primary criterion for undergraduate entry. GPA becomes relevant mainly when international students apply with alternative qualifications or when applying for postgraduate study.

Can a low GPA be offset for UK postgraduate applications?

UK postgraduate admissions are often more flexible than US programmes. Relevant work experience, a strong personal statement, a high dissertation/thesis grade, or professional qualifications can offset a borderline GPA. Some programmes also offer conditional offers or a pre-Master's pathway for students whose GPA falls below the standard threshold.
