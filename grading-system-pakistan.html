Pakistan Grading System: CGPA and the HEC Scale

How the HEC-standard 4.0 CGPA scale works in Pakistan, how percentage maps to grade points, and how to convert Pakistani grades for study abroad applications.

Pakistan's university grading system

Pakistani universities accredited by the Higher Education Commission (HEC) predominantly use a credit-hour based semester system with a 4.0 CGPA scale — directly compatible with the US system for the purpose of international comparisons.

Semester vs. annual system

The HEC actively promotes the semester-CGPA system, and it is now standard at most universities. However, some affiliated colleges and professional programs (particularly older medical and law colleges) still operate on annual examinations with percentage marks. Conversion between the two requires mapping percentage bands to GPA via your institution's official table.

Converting Pakistani grades for international applications

Frequently asked questions

What GPA scale does Pakistan use?

Pakistani HEC-accredited universities use a 4.0 GPA scale for CGPA calculation, modelled closely on the US system. The letter grade to grade-point mapping is nearly identical, with an A earning 4.0 and the pass threshold typically set at a D (1.0) or a minimum CGPA of 2.0.

How does the percentage system relate to CGPA in Pakistan?

Many Pakistani universities calculate a percentage from CGPA using the formula: Percentage ≈ (CGPA / 4.0) × 100 × scaling factor, though individual institutions may vary. The simpler direct formula CGPA ÷ 4.0 × 100 gives a rough percentage. For a more precise conversion, use the CGPA Calculator with your actual marks.

What is the HEC grading policy in Pakistan?

The Higher Education Commission (HEC) recommends that universities maintain a minimum CGPA of 2.0 for a student to remain in good standing, with 2.0 CGPA required for degree conferral. Individual universities may set higher departmental requirements, particularly for engineering and medical programs.

Does Pakistan use semester or annual grading?

Most modern HEC-accredited universities use a semester system with CGPA, especially in engineering, business, and IT programs. Some older universities and professional colleges still use an annual examination system with percentage marks rather than CGPA.
