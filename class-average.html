Statistics Explained

Mean: The arithmetic average of all scores.

Median: The middle score when sorted — better for skewed distributions.

Standard Deviation: How spread out the scores are. Low SD = tight cluster; high SD = wide spread.

Why use median instead of mean?

If a few students score very high or very low, the mean gets pulled toward those outliers. The median is more representative of a typical student's performance.

What is a good class average?

Most instructors aim for a class average around 70–80%. Averages below 60% may indicate the assessment was too difficult; above 90% may indicate it was too easy.
