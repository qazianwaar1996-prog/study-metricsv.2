The GPA Scale, Explained

What a 4.0 GPA actually means, how letter grades map to numbers, and the exact formula used to calculate it — in plain English.

What is the GPA scale?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a single number — typically between 0.0 and 4.0 in the US — that summarises your academic performance by converting letter grades into numbers, multiplying by credit hours, and dividing by total credits attempted.

How letter grades map to grade points

The standard US conversion table is:

Letter Grade Percentage Range Grade Points
A / A+93–100%4.0
A−90–92%3.7
B+87–89%3.3
B83–86%3.0
B−80–82%2.7
C+77–79%2.3
C73–76%2.0
D60–69%1.0
F0–59%0.0

The GPA formula

For each course, multiply its grade points by the number of credit hours. Sum all of those products, then divide by the total credit hours attempted:

GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credit Hours) ÷ Σ Credit Hours

Example: an A (4.0) in a 3-credit course and a B (3.0) in a 4-credit course gives (4.0×3 + 3.0×4) ÷ (3+4) = (12+12) ÷ 7 = 3.43.

Weighted vs. unweighted GPA

Unweighted GPA: every course uses the same 4.0 scale regardless of difficulty. Weighted GPA: honors, AP, or IB courses earn extra grade points, so a 4.0 ceiling no longer applies. High-school contexts often use weighted GPA; university transcripts almost always use unweighted.

What is a "good" GPA?

Context matters. A 3.5 is excellent at most US universities. Competitive graduate programs often expect 3.7 or above. Employers typically screen for 3.0 as a baseline. Internationally, a 3.0 US GPA translates roughly to a UK 2:1 or a German 2.3 — use the GPA Converter for your country.

Frequently asked questions

What does a 4.0 GPA mean?

A 4.0 is the highest possible GPA on the standard US scale. It means you received an A (90–100%) in every credit-bearing course. The number comes from the grade-point value assigned to each letter: A = 4.0, B = 3.0, C = 2.0, D = 1.0, F = 0.

Can you have a GPA above 4.0?

Yes — on a weighted scale. Many high schools award bonus grade points for honors, AP, or IB courses (an A in an AP class might count as 5.0 instead of 4.0). College transcripts almost always use the unweighted 4.0 scale, so GPAs above 4.0 are rare in higher education.

How do plus and minus grades affect GPA?

A+ and A are both worth 4.0 in most systems, but A− is 3.7, B+ is 3.3, B is 3.0, B− is 2.7, and so on. Some schools do not use plus/minus grading — check your institution's grade-point table before calculating.

What is the minimum GPA to graduate?

Most US universities require a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 (a C average) to earn a degree. Individual programs, especially competitive ones like engineering or nursing, often require 2.5 or 3.0. Graduate schools typically require 3.0.
