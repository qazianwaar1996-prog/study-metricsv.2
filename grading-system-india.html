India Grading System: CGPA and the 10-Point Scale

How India's 10-point CGPA scale works, the UGC ×9.5 percentage formula, and how to convert Indian grades for graduate school applications abroad.

India's 10-point CGPA system

Following UGC (University Grants Commission) guidelines, most Indian universities now use a 10-point CGPA scale, though percentage-based grading remains common in older affiliated colleges and boards. The two systems coexist, and conversion between them is frequently required.

Converting CGPA to percentage (UGC formula)

The UGC-recommended formula used by most central and autonomous universities:

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

Use the GPA to Percentage Calculator or the Global GPA Converter for instant conversion and a printable result for overseas applications.

CBSE and school-level grading

The CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education) uses a separate 10-point scale for Classes 9–10 where grades are assigned in bands: A1 (91–100) = 10, A2 (81–90) = 9, B1 (71–80) = 8, B2 (61–70) = 7, and so on. This scale is not identical to the university CGPA system.

Frequently asked questions

How is CGPA converted to percentage in India?

The most widely used formula, recommended by UGC and followed by most central universities, is: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. So a CGPA of 8.5 converts to 8.5 × 9.5 = 80.75%. Some universities use their own multipliers — always verify with your institution's official conversion table.

What is the difference between CGPA and percentage in Indian universities?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the credit-weighted average of grade points on a 10-point scale. Percentage is the traditional marks-out-of-100 system. The UGC ×9.5 formula provides an approximate conversion, but the two systems are not perfectly interchangeable because percentage systems track raw marks, while CGPA uses grade bands.

What CGPA is required for a distinction in India?

Definitions vary by university, but a common standard is: Distinction ≥ 7.5 CGPA (approximately 71.25%), First Class 6.0–7.49, Second Class 5.0–5.99, Pass 4.0–4.99, Fail below 4.0.

How does a 10-point CGPA convert to a US 4.0 GPA?

A rough linear conversion divides by 2.5 (e.g., 8.0 ÷ 2.5 = 3.2 GPA), but this oversimplifies. The Global GPA Converter uses calibrated tables that account for the grade-band compression in the Indian system and produces a more accurate result for graduate school applications.
