Final Exam Prep: The Numbers-First Checklist

Work out the exact score you need before you open a textbook — then build your study plan around that number.

Step 1: Calculate the score you actually need

Before opening a textbook, use the Final Exam Calculator to find the minimum score you need on the final to hit your target grade. This single number changes how you prioritise:

  • If you need 60%: a broad review of all topics is better than depth on any one area.
  • If you need 85%+: depth on high-weight topics and past exam patterns is worth more than coverage.
  • If you need 100%+: the math says it is impossible — refocus energy on extra credit or other courses.

Step 2: Understand what the final is worth

Exam weight dramatically affects how much study effort is justified. A 20%-weighted final in a course where you hold 88% affects your grade far less than a 50%-weighted final in a course where you hold 70%. Calculate both before deciding where to invest your study hours.

Step 3: Map topics to likely exam weight

Review past exams, syllabus weighting, and any study guide your instructor provided. Score each topic on two axes: likelihood of appearing and your current confidence. Study first where likelihood is high and confidence is low.

Step 4: Build a time budget with the Study Schedule Generator

Once you know how many days you have and which topics need the most work, the Study Schedule Generator distributes your available hours across topics. Aim for sessions of 25–50 minutes with short breaks — the same rhythm the Pomodoro Timer enforces automatically.

Step 5: Use active recall, not passive re-reading

Closing the textbook and writing down everything you remember about a topic — then checking — is consistently more effective for exam retention than re-reading. Practice problems and past-paper questions under timed conditions outperform highlighting by a wide margin.

Quick checklist

  • ☐ Calculate the exact score needed (Final Exam Calculator)
  • ☐ Check the current standing in every course (Grade Predictor)
  • ☐ Rank topics by exam likelihood × your knowledge gap
  • ☐ Block study sessions in a schedule (Study Schedule)
  • ☐ Use the Pomodoro Timer for each session
  • ☐ Review the previous session's material at the start of each new one (spaced recall)
  • ☐ Do at least one past paper under exam conditions

Frequently asked questions

What score do I need on my final exam to pass?

Use the Final Exam Calculator: enter your current grade, the weight of the final, and the minimum passing grade. It outputs the exact score required. Knowing this number first tells you whether you need to aim for perfection or can focus effort selectively.

How do I calculate what final exam score I need for an A?

Required Final Score = (Target Grade − Current Grade × Current Weight) ÷ Final Weight. For example, if you have 78% going into a 40%-weighted final and want an 85% overall: (85 − 78 × 0.6) ÷ 0.4 = (85 − 46.8) ÷ 0.4 = 95.5%. The Final Exam Calculator handles this automatically.

How many days before an exam should I start studying?

Research on spaced practice suggests spacing study sessions over at least a week produces better long-term retention than equivalent time crammed into one or two days. For a high-stakes final, starting two to three weeks out with the Study Schedule Generator allows time to space and review each topic multiple times.

How much study time do I actually need per credit hour?

A common academic guideline is two to three hours of outside study per credit hour per week. For a 3-credit course, that is 6–9 hours per week. The Study Time Calculator personalises this estimate based on your target grade and difficulty.
