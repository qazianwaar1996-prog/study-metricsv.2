UK Grading System: Degree Classifications Explained

How UK First/2:1/2:2/Third degree classifications are calculated, what the percentage bands mean, and how they convert to a US GPA.

UK degree classification system

UK universities award undergraduate degrees in classifications rather than a numeric GPA. The classification is determined by your overall percentage mark, which is itself a weighted average of module marks across one or more years (typically the final two years carry most weight).

How the classification is calculated

Most universities weight the final year at 60–80% of the overall mark, with the penultimate year making up the remainder. Some institutions use a 'borderline' rule where a candidate within 2% of the next classification boundary may be promoted if a majority of their modules fall in the higher band.

Postgraduate (Master's) grades

UK Master's programmes typically award: Distinction (70%+), Merit (60–69%), Pass (50–59%), and Fail (below 50%). These are separate from the undergraduate classification system.

Frequently asked questions

What is a First-Class Honours degree in GPA terms?

A First (70%+) is broadly equivalent to a 3.7–4.0 on the US 4.0 scale, though exact conversions vary by evaluator. WES (World Education Services) and NARIC typically map a First to 3.7+ GPA. The GPA Converter gives the conversion your evaluator is most likely to apply.

Is a 2:1 a good degree?

Yes. A 2:1 (Upper Second Class, 60–69%) is the standard 'good degree' benchmark in UK graduate recruitment and postgraduate admissions. Most UK employer graduate schemes require a 2:1 as a minimum. It maps to roughly 3.0–3.7 GPA on the US scale.

Do Scottish universities use the same classification system?

Scotland uses a similar system but with different grade bands: Distinction (70%+), Merit (60–69%), Pass (50–59%), and a separate honours classification for integrated master's-level programmes. Older Scottish degree traditions may refer to distinctions differently — always check the issuing university.

How are UK percentages different from US percentages?

In the UK, 70% is an excellent mark (a First); 80–90% is exceptional and rare. In the US, 90%+ is an A and 70% is a C. Never compare raw percentages across systems — always convert to the destination country's grade classification.
