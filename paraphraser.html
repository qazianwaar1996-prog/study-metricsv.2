How to write robust academic copy

Paraphrasing is more than swapping words for synonyms. To produce scholarly writing, you must adjust the sentence architecture and logical progression of ideas. High-quality paraphrasing preserves your source's integrity while eliminating redundant phrasing and maintaining an objective, critical voice.

What do the different paraphrasing modes do?

Academic: Adapts your writing to a style fit for peer-reviewed journals, focusing on precision, passive voice structures where appropriate, and formal syntax.
Formal: Elevates tone to standard professional standard. Great for cover letters and emails.
Simple: Strips down jargon and unnecessary fluff to maximize readability.
Concise: Condenses long-winded arguments into short, impactful assertions.
Expand: Helps flesh out raw bullet points into complete, detailed academic paragraphs.

Is my data stored or sent to external servers?
