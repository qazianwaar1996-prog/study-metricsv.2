Australian Grading System: HD, Distinction, Credit and Pass

How Australia's High Distinction through Pass grading system works, the percentage bands each grade covers, and how to convert Australian grades to a US 4.0 GPA.

Australia's university grading system

Australian universities use a five-level grading system with standardised labels — High Distinction (HD), Distinction (D), Credit (C), Pass (P), and Fail (F) — though the exact percentage cutoffs differ slightly between institutions.

Important: "Distinction" ≠ "Distinction" internationally

The word "Distinction" means different things in different systems. An Australian D (Distinction, 75–84%) is not the same as a UK Master's Distinction (70%+) or a US A−. When presenting your Australian grades internationally, always provide the percentage mark alongside the grade label, and use the Global GPA Converter to supply the converted GPA.

Weighted Average Mark (WAM)

Many Australian universities calculate a Weighted Average Mark (WAM) in addition to or instead of a GPA. WAM is the credit-weighted average of all percentage marks — a more granular number than a letter-grade GPA. Some graduate programs and employers specifically request WAM; others prefer GPA. Both may appear on your transcript.

Frequently asked questions

What does HD mean in Australian grading?

HD stands for High Distinction, the highest grade in the Australian university system, typically awarded for marks of 85% or above (the exact cutoff varies by institution — some use 80% for HD). It is roughly equivalent to an A on the US letter-grade scale (4.0 GPA).

How does Australian grading convert to a US GPA?

A common conversion is: HD ≈ 4.0, D (Distinction) ≈ 3.3–3.7, C (Credit) ≈ 2.7–3.0, P (Pass) ≈ 2.0, F (Fail) = 0. WES and other credential evaluators may use slightly different mappings. Use the Global GPA Converter for the conversion your evaluator is likely to apply.

Is a Credit a bad grade in Australia?

No. A Credit (typically 65–74%) is a respectable result in the Australian system — it is above average. Many students misread it as equivalent to a US 'C' (average), but the two are not the same. An Australian Credit corresponds roughly to a US B or B+.

Do all Australian universities use the same grade boundaries?

No. While the HD/D/C/P/F label system is standard, the percentage cutoffs vary by institution. For example, Melbourne uses 80% for HD while some other universities use 85%. Always verify the official grade scale from the transcript-issuing institution.
